Korba Coal Field

The Korba Coal Field, located in Chhattisgarh, is one of India's largest and most significant coal-producing regions. Managed primarily by South Eastern Coalfields Limited (SECL), a subsidiary of Coal India Ltd, Korba contributes significantly to the country's energy needs, producing over 16% of India's coal annually.

The coal field hosts some of India's largest open-cast mines, including Gevra, Kusmunda, and Dipka. However, the region faces challenges due to aging coal mines, environmental degradation, and socio-economic dependencies on the coal economy. Efforts are underway to plan a sustainable transition for the region.
